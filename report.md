Phylogenetic Analysis Report - Enoch
I analyzed three cystovirus genes (P1, P3, P12) using nucleotide sequences. After filtering, the dataset included 78 (P1), 138 (P3), and 234 (P12) sequences. P1: Major inner capsid protein; P3: Attachment spike protein and P12: Essential for envelope assembly.
Gene trees and a concatenated tree were inferred using IQ-TREE, and a species tree was constructed using ASTRAL. The results showed strong disagreement between methods. concatenated tree differed substantially from all gene trees (RF ~0.54–0.67), while the species tree closely matched the P3 gene tree (RF ~0.056). This indicates that coalescent methods better capture gene-specific evolutionary patterns.
Furthermore, there was also discordance seen among genes, with P1 and P12 showing complete agreement (RF = 0) and P3 differing strongly from both (RF ~0.6), consistent with different evolutionary histories across segments in cystoviruses. However, since P2 and p12 are on different segments and have different function, so I am not sure why they seem to be identical in their evolutionary history. Seems to be an error to me.
My major challenge was the uneven sequence representation across genes and conflicting topologies. Quantifying differences using RF distances helped to address this issue. Overall, the project helped me highlight that gene trees are discordant and the coalescent method is an interesting and more realistic representation of cystovirus evolution
